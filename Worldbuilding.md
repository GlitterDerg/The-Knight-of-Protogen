## Living Species

Due to the great merging event, two worlds which were initially apart got merged into a singular world, pulling its residents from both side into the new world.

The first world consisted of anthropomorphic species.

The second world consisted of humans and other animal species.

Synthetic species were born from the collaboration of humans and anthropomorphic species to create a species that will surpass both of them.

### Beastmen (Furry/Kemono/Anthropomorphic Animals)

- Fox : An anthropomorphic species, varies in region, most common appearance being a small canine with a big fluffy tail. The eastern variants (kitsune) are an exception of this, with one can possess up to nine tails at once, their culture also made them stands out among the rest of the species too.

- Canine : It is both surprising and amusing that this species shares a common ancestor with the reptilian kobolds, once told in the legends that the original group split apart following two different gods, leading to their distinct evolution. This species has varying heights and appearance among its members (due to many sub-species) and appears mostly friendly and loyal to those kind to them.

(The fact that Canine and Kobold shared the same species was heavily inspired by the design of kobolds in DnD1e where they have a canine-like appearance, before gradually shifting towards a more reptilian appearance that we're used to nowadays.)

- Feline: Another species whose population rivals that of Canine. Their distinct appearance being their pointy ears, whiskers, tail, and their unique 'meow' sound that they make. Known to be thinkers and inventors, as research has found out that the average IQ of this species surpasses other species by a meager degree, leading to most of them becoming smart.

- Dragonborns : An ancient species found to have lived since the dusk of the pre-historic era, through a great calamity wiped out many sub-species of them, leaving a few to survive in the present world. There exists two common species of dragons. Eastern and Western dragons, with their distinct features being their wings, which eastern dragons lack. The culture between these two species are also vastly different, while the Eastern dragons sought peace and often pratice pacifist methods, the Western dragons sought strength and bacome battle-hardened warriors instead. Though many of those who are considered 'outliers' exists somewhere...

- Kobold - Once shared a common ancestor with the canines. Over time this species become loyal devotees to the Dragonborns due to their long time partnership since ancient times. This species is often seen as mischeivious and not so bright minded to other species other than Dragonborns, which their short appearence might be contributed to this view. In the present day, some members of this species strive to be independent and hope that one day they'll deserve the right kind of respect from other species.
